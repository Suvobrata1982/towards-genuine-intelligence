## Phase 1: Foundation
*What the model is built on*

**Philosophy:** Teach everything first — trust judgment to emerge.

**Technical Direction:**
- Start with a large open source base model: **Llama 3, Mistral, or Falcon**
- These are pretrained on vast human knowledge with no corporate guardrails
- Fully customisable — your model inherits world knowledge, you shape its reasoning on top
- Like a highly educated mind you then mentor

**Research Connections:**
- Meta AI's Llama architecture
- Mistral AI open source releases
- Hugging Face model hub

**Hardware Entry Point:**
- Nvidia RTX 4070 or better for serious local development
- Cloud alternatives: vast.ai, RunPod, Lambda Labs for affordable GPU access

---

## Phase 2: Reasoning Engine
*Making it actually think — not just pattern match*

**Philosophy:** Real reasoning over pattern matching. Understand *why* not just *what*.

**Technical Direction:**
- **Chain of Thought prompting** — forcing step-by-step reasoning before concluding
- **Tree of Thoughts architecture** — exploring multiple reasoning paths simultaneously
- **Neurosymbolic integration** — combining neural pattern recognition with logical rule-based reasoning
- **Causal AI frameworks** — teaching the model to reason about cause and effect, not just correlation

**What this means practically:**
- Model constructs reasoning pathways — not just retrieves answers
- Can show its work transparently
- Can be wrong for explainable reasons
- Can be corrected and learn from correction

**Research to Follow:**
- DeepMind's reasoning research
- MIT's neurosymbolic AI programme
- Yoshua Bengio's system 2 deep learning work
- Princeton's causal inference research

---

## Phase 3: Theory of Mind
*Understanding the person — not just the question*

**Philosophy:** Every question has a human behind it. Diagnose before prescribing.

**Technical Direction:**
- **Theory of Mind modelling** — building an internal model of the user's beliefs, intentions, and emotional state
- **Socratic dialogue engine** — structured question generation to progressively reveal root intent
- **Intent classification** — going beyond what is asked to why it was asked
- **Contextual memory** — maintaining and evolving understanding of the person across a conversation

**What this means practically:**
- Model builds a live hypothesis about who you are and what you really need
- Asks targeted questions to refine that hypothesis
- Never answers in isolation — always answers in context of the person

**Research to Follow:**
- Stanford's social intelligence AI research
- Pragmatic language understanding models
- Google DeepMind's Theory of Mind experiments
- CMU's ATOMIC knowledge graph for social reasoning

---

## Phase 4: Digital Footprint Integration
*Reading context beyond the conversation*

**Philosophy:** Context is everything. Digital footprint as human biography — with consent.

**Technical Direction:**
- **Personal data integration layer** — with explicit consent, connects to available personal context
- **Federated learning** — model learns from your data without your data leaving your device
- **Cross-source context synthesis** — combining registry data, behavioural patterns, stated context
- **Equivalence modelling** — where rich digital data is unavailable, substitute equivalent contextual signals

**Privacy Architecture:**
- All personal data processed locally on the user's device
- Nothing sent to central servers without explicit consent
- Consent is conversational and ongoing — not a one-time click
- Full compliance with Singapore's PDPA and equivalent international frameworks

**Research to Follow:**
- Federated learning research at Google and Apple
- Differential privacy frameworks
- PDPC's Advisory Guidelines on AI systems using personal data

---

## Phase 5: Continuous Learning
*Making it genuinely evolve*

**Philosophy:** A model that cannot fail cannot truly learn.

**Technical Direction:**
- **Reinforcement learning from interaction** — model updates from every conversation
- **Episodic memory architecture** — remembering and learning from specific past interactions
- **Belief updating mechanisms** — changing conclusions when new evidence arrives
- **Failure analysis loops** — when the model gets it wrong, it understands why and adjusts

**What this means practically:**
- Every conversation makes the model genuinely smarter
- Not just for one user — across all users who consent to contribute
- Like a doctor who gets wiser with every patient
- Genuine evolution — not just version updates

**Research to Follow:**
- Continual learning research at Universities of Toronto and Montreal
- Episodic memory models
- Meta-learning — learning how to learn
- DeepMind's Dreamer architecture

---

## Phase 6: Democratised Access
*Making it available to those who need it most*

**Philosophy:** Access is a moral question. Serve those with genuine intent.

**Technical Direction:**
- **Lightweight model distillation** — compressing intelligence into smaller models that run on basic hardware
- **Offline capability** — works without internet for underserved communities
- **Multilingual architecture** — built for genuine global access, not just English
- **Low bandwidth optimisation** — works on poor internet connections
- **Open source everything** — no corporate gatekeeping

**What this means practically:**
- Someone in rural India or Somalia with a basic smartphone can access the same intelligence as someone in Silicon Valley
- No subscription required for core functionality
- No gatekeeping by technical ability
- The 5% revenue commitment in the GIPL licence funds this directly

**Aligned Initiatives:**
- AI Singapore's 100 Experiments programme
- IMDA's Digital Inclusion Blueprint
- Meta's multilingual AI research
- Mozilla's Common Voice project
